Kentucky Dams in Relation to Counties and River Basins

I decided to create my Kentucky Dams in Relation to Counties and River basins map because I was interested to see how river basins compared to each other in amount of dams. Kentucky has the most navigable waterbodies second only to Alaska. There are many maps created to show and research Kentucky's watersheds so I decided to map dams in relation to river basins and counties.

Link to high resolution version

This map shows regulated dams within Kentucky's river basins. The viewer should be able to see where higher concentrations of dams are found in the different river basins.

Link to high resolution version

From this map you can see where dams are in relation to counties and see which river basins have a higher amount of dams than other basins.

Link to high resolution version

This map is zoomed in on the Licking River basin and the Big Sandy/Little Sandy and Tygarts Creek basin. I chose to display these basins because I live in the Licking River basin and I work in the Big Sandy/Little Sandy and Tygarts Creek basin. I was interested to see the varying amounts of dams in each basin. I am very interested in watersheds and watersheds drain into major river basins. Because of this, I was interested to see if there was a relationship between the number of dams in certain river basins.
